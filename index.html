My Journey Into Programming

My journey into programming did not start with a clear roadmap. At the beginning, I was driven mainly by curiosity and the desire to understand how applications and websites actually work behind the scenes.

January 4, 2026

Discovering the Basics

I began with HTML and CSS, learning how structure and styling come together to form a webpage. Simple layouts, spacing issues, and responsiveness were challenging at first, but seeing visual results kept me motivated.

January 7, 2026

Facing JavaScript

JavaScript was a major turning point. Concepts like variables, conditions, loops, and functions felt abstract initially, especially when combined with DOM manipulation. I often struggled to understand why things didn’t work as expected.

January 9, 2026

Learning Through Projects

Building small projects changed everything. Projects like a restaurant ordering app helped me understand state, events, and dynamic UI updates. Mistakes became lessons, and debugging became a daily habit rather than a fear.

January 10, 2026

Improving My Thinking

Over time, I noticed my approach to problems improving. I started breaking problems into smaller parts, writing cleaner logic, and refactoring code instead of rewriting everything from scratch.

January 11, 2026

Where I Am Today

Today, I don’t consider myself an expert, but I am confident in my learning process. I understand how to search, experiment, fail, and improve. Programming has become less about memorizing syntax and more about logical thinking and problem solving.
